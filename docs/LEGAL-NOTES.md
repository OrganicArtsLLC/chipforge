# Legal Notes — Copyright & Original Works

## Core Principle

**ChipForge generates all audio from mathematical synthesis. No audio samples, recordings, or copyrighted sound files are used or distributed.**

Every sound in every song is computed from scratch using:
- Waveform generators (sine, square, saw, etc.)
- Karplus-Strong physical modeling
- FM synthesis algorithms
- Formant vocal synthesis
- ADSR envelopes
- Digital signal processing (filters, reverb, compression)

No portion of any original recording is present in the output.

## Song Arrangements: Derivative Works Analysis

### What ChipForge Songs Are

The songs in this repository are **original electronic arrangements** inspired by well-known compositions. They are:

1. **New performances** — every note is placed by the arrangement author (human + AI), not extracted from recordings
2. **Different instrumentation** — chip tune / electronic synthesis, not the original instruments
3. **Often significantly altered** — different tempo, key, arrangement, structure, duration
4. **Original synthesis** — the timbres themselves are novel (no sample libraries)

### What They Are NOT

- They are NOT recordings of the original works
- They are NOT samples or loops from the originals
- They do NOT contain any audio from the original artists
- They do NOT use sample packs or commercial instrument libraries

### Fair Use / Transformative Considerations

Many of these arrangements fall under transformative use because:
- The medium is entirely different (electronic synthesis vs. original recording)
- The purpose is educational/creative exploration of music theory
- The arrangements are often substantially different from the originals
- Many source works are in the public domain (classical pieces)

### Categories

**Public Domain (no copyright concern):**
- All classical pieces (Beethoven, Bach, Vivaldi, Debussy, Ravel, Morricone)
- Traditional melodies

**Arrangements of Copyrighted Melodies:**
- These reproduce the *melody* (a sequence of notes) but not the *recording*
- Melody copyright is held by the songwriter/publisher
- For commercial use, mechanical licenses may be required
- For personal/educational use, this is generally permissible
- The ChipForge arrangements are often different enough in form, speed, and presentation to be considered transformative

### Pixel Vault Integration

When using ChipForge songs in Pixel Vault games:
- **Public domain compositions** — free to use commercially
- **Original compositions** (Neon Trance, Digital Aurora, Voices from Code, etc.) — owned by the creator, free to use
- **Arrangements of copyrighted melodies** — assess on a case-by-case basis:
  - Many are altered sufficiently in tempo, key, instrumentation, and arrangement
  - The synthesis itself is entirely original (no samples)
  - For commercial game distribution, consider:
    - Using only original compositions and public domain arrangements
    - Obtaining mechanical licenses for recognizable melody arrangements
    - Further altering arrangements to increase transformative distance

### Best Practices for Commercial Use

1. **Prefer original compositions** — ChipForge can generate unlimited original music
2. **Use public domain sources** — classical, traditional, folk melodies
3. **Alter arrangements significantly** — change key, tempo, structure, add/remove sections
4. **Document the transformation** — keep notes on how each arrangement differs from the source
5. **When in doubt, get a license** — mechanical licenses for cover arrangements are standardized and affordable
